Background

With a BSc in Theoretical Physics and an MSc in Quantum Fields and Fundamental Forces under my belt, you'd think I was destined to be the next Einstein. But plot twist - I ended up in game development!

Back in university, I took an Object-Oriented Programming module in C++. For my final project, I decided to create a custom ASCII game engine. That experience lit a fire in me for game dev, setting the stage for what was to come.

By the end of my master's program, I was still fascinated by software development and thought, “Why not make it my job?” So, I jumped into the world of software development, spending five years mostly focused on web dev. I polished my skills and discovered that code can be just as thrilling as solving a physics problem - most of the time, anyway!

In one of those years, I participated in Ludum Dare, an experience that sparked my passion for game dev all over again. Now, armed with a solid grasp of the Unity engine, I'm thrilled to be working on my first commercial title, Turtle vs Rabbit, a sokoban puzzle game that promises to be a fun ride - unless, of course, you're a turtle trying to outrun a rabbit!
